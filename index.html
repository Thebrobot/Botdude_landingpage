What you are actually buying

What is an AI agent or AI receptionist for a home services business?

On every ring it should do real work: answer in your voice, qualify the job, book or capture the lead, text back fast, and route emergencies the way you said. That is what we ship as an AI receptionist (also called an AI phone agent). Agent Broski is our first agent for HVAC, plumbing, roofing, and similar trades so fewer calls die in voicemail.

How does Agent Broski work on a real phone call?

When a call hits your line or a forwarded or overflow path, Agent Broski answers with your business rules, qualifies the caller, books into your calendar where integrated, texts the lead when you want that path, and escalates when the job is outside the script. We customize setup for your pricing, service area, and how you want emergencies handled.

Do I need to change my business phone number to use an AI receptionist?

Usually no. Many businesses keep their main published number and forward unanswered, after-hours, or overflow calls to Agent Broski. We’ll walk you through the right routing for your carrier so trucks, ads, and Google Business Profile stay consistent.

Trust, disclosure & call spikes

Will callers know they are talking to an AI?

With a tuned voice and script, many callers focus on getting help fast, not on whether the voice is human. What matters is accurate booking and follow-up. If you want disclosure language in the greeting for your market, we can set that up.

What happens if the AI cannot answer a question or it is an emergency?

You define escalation: transfer rules, dispatch lines, or structured messages that go straight to your team. The goal is zero dead ends, either a booked job, a qualified lead, or a routed urgent call instead of voicemail.

Can an AI receptionist answer calls 24/7?

Yes. AI phone coverage does not sleep, so after-hours and weekend rings can be handled with the same booking and qualification logic you use in business hours, without paying for a separate night and weekend shift.

Can AI handle multiple inbound calls at the same time?

Unlike a single human receptionist, an AI agent can take many simultaneous calls, which helps when marketing, weather, or seasonality spikes call volume and your office line stacks up.

Integrations, transfers & alternatives

Can AI integrate with ServiceTitan, Google Calendar, or other tools?

Most businesses keep their published number and use call forwarding from the carrier, a CallRail tracking pool, or a PBX so unanswered, after-hours, or overflow paths reach Agent Broski. We connect calendars and job systems where your stack allows, including Google Calendar and platforms like ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro, and similar CRM or field tools when APIs and permissions support it. Exact integrations depend on your accounts; we confirm everything before go-live.

Can AI transfer calls to me or my team?

Yes, when your routing supports it. We set rules for which calls Broski handles end-to-end versus which should roll to dispatch, on-call, or ownership based on urgency and time of day.

How is an AI answering service different from a traditional answering service?

Classic answering services often use human operators with scripts, queues, and per-minute billing. An AI agent answers instantly, scales with call volume, runs 24/7, and can sync booking and follow-up to your process, usually at a predictable monthly cost instead of unpredictable per-minute fees.

Your team & economics

Is this trying to replace my office staff or dispatcher?

No, that is the wrong frame. Agent Broski is built to add coverage: overflow calls, after-hours, and speed-to-lead while your people focus on customers in front of them and jobs in progress. Many businesses use him alongside a great CSR.

We already have someone answering phones, why would we add AI?

One person cannot be in two places at once, and you still lose money when the fourth call rolls to voicemail. AI scales first response so more of your marketing and referral spend turns into booked work.

How much does AI reception cost compared to hiring a full-time receptionist?

A full-time receptionist can cost tens of thousands per year in salary alone, plus benefits, turnover, and coverage gaps.
